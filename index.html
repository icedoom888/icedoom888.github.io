ArtWalks

via Latent Diffusion Models

In this highly artistic project, I implemented an end-to-end AI tool for image interpolation that enables video generations from static pictures.
Do you want to go from a Van Gogh to a Monet? From a Picasso to a picture of your grandma? With ArtWalks you can.
During the realisation of this project, I deployed state-of-the-art techniques in image and video generation such as diffusion based generative models and large motion interpolation networks.
The project came to life as a collaboration with artist Georges Burki.
ArtWalks has been used in the production of multiple art-piece videos that have been shown within an immersive concert setting accompanied by the music the artist.

AvatarForge

With AvatarForge I developed a fully open-source pipeline for Digital Avatar Generation. Provide a 3 minute video recording of yourself and train your own digital twin. The pipeline is completely open-source and can be accessed via Docker container. Within AvatarForge, I offer two separate models for avatar generation:

  • A puppeteering model where only the mouth region is generated, aligned with the new audio, and then pasted over the original video.
  • A head-motion model where the full half-body avatar is generated, and both mouth and head motion are aligned with the input audio signal.

Within the realisation of this project we launched a deep-fake awareness initiative in Swiss schools in collaboration with SRF to prevent divulgation of fake news.Moreover, this technology has been used in the production of an art piece video in collaboration with ETH AI Center and artist Nora Al-Badri, which will be displayed in 2024 @KW Museum in Berlin.

REAL-DIBS

Rendering Alternative Deep Image Based Sansoucci

During my master thesis, I build upon new advances in neural networks and differentiable rendering to synthesize novel views based on an initial 3D reconstruction and the original input views. I proposed a novel hybrid method called Morph-GAN that combines advantages through per vertex geometry refinement with the texture generation capabilities of GAN models within a differentiable rendering framework. Our experiments show that the proposed model is capable of significantly improving image quality and the overall realism of generated images.
